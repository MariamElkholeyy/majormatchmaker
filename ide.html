Industrial Engineering and Systems Management:

It is the core principle of Industrial and Systems Engineering (IEM) centers around solving problems in multiple domains, including logistics, business, engineering, etc., backed by scientific approaches in data analytics, systems modelling, decision-making and management.

IEM Program at E-JUST:

  • DATA ANALYTICS AND DECISION MAKING.
  • COMPUTATIONAL AND MODELLING SKILLS.
  • PROJECT AND OPERATIONS MANAGEMENT.
  • SYSTEMS ENGINEERING AND ANALYSIS.

Career Prospects:

IEM graduates have a useful set of analytical, computing, and managerial skills that can be applied across multiple industries. This allows them to:

  • Seek out the best paying jobs.
  • Be protected against industry-specific shocks and trends.
  • Find employment in an industry that they are interested in.

Mechatronics and Robotics Engineering:

Students learn to design and control systems merging mechanical components with electronics and computing. This multidisciplinary approach equips graduates for careers in various industries. They work on diverse projects, from manufacturing automation to robotics and aerospace technology. With expertise in mechanical design, electronics, programming, and control systems, mechatronics engineers drive innovation in fields like autonomous vehicles and smart systems. They play crucial roles in advancing technology and solving complex engineering challenges.

MTR Program at E-JUST:

  • Introduction to Mechatronics.
  • Mechanical Design and Analysis.
  • Electrical Circuits and Electronics.
  • Digital Logic and Microcontrollers.
  • Programming for Engineers.
  • Control Systems Engineering.
  • Robotics and Automation.
  • Sensors and Actuators.
  • Signal Processing.
  • System Dynamics and Modeling.
  • Mechatronics Laboratory.

Career Prospects:

Mechatronics engineering offers diverse career opportunities across various industries, including:

  • Manufacturing: Designing and optimizing automated production processes and systems.
  • Robotics: Developing robots for industrial, medical, or service applications.
  • Automotive: Designing and implementing electronic systems for vehicles, including autonomous driving technology.
  • Aerospace: Working on control systems for aircraft, spacecraft, and drones.
  • Biomedical Engineering: Designing medical devices and systems, such as prosthetics and diagnostic equipment.
  • Renewable Energy: Developing systems for harvesting and storing renewable energy, like solar and wind power.

Materials Science and Engineering:

It draws from chemistry, physics, and engineering to understand how materials are manufactured, processed, and used in various applications. Material engineers work across industries such as aerospace, automotive, electronics, and biomedical, contributing to innovations in areas like lightweight materials, renewable energy, and medical devices. They play a crucial role in advancing technology, improving product performance, and addressing global challenges by creating materials with enhanced properties and functionality.

  • Introduction to Materials Science and Engineering.
  • Materials Chemistry.
  • Materials Physics.
  • Mechanical Behavior of Materials.
  • Thermodynamics of Materials.
  • Material Characterization Techniques.
  • Materials Processing and Manufacturing.
  • Electronic Properties of Materials.
  • Biomaterials.
  • Nanomaterials.
  • Composite Materials.
  • Materials Selection and Design.
  • Corrosion and Degradation of Materials.
  • Materials for Energy Applications.

Career Prospects:

Career prospects for materials engineers are promising, with opportunities available across a wide range of industries. Some common career paths and job roles include:

  • Materials Engineer: Working in research and development to design and develop new materials with specific properties for various applications.
  • Process Engineer: Overseeing the manufacturing and processing of materials to ensure quality, efficiency, and cost-effectiveness.
  • Quality Control Engineer: Testing and inspecting materials to ensure they meet industry standards and specifications.
  • Materials Scientist: Conducting research to understand the fundamental properties and behavior of materials and develop new materials with unique characteristics.
  • Product Development Engineer: Collaborating with interdisciplinary teams to design and optimize products using advanced materials.
  • Aerospace Engineer: Designing and testing materials for use in aircraft, spacecraft, and satellite systems.
  • Biomedical Engineer: Developing biomaterials for medical implants, tissue engineering, and drug delivery systems.
  • Environmental Engineer: Working on sustainable materials and processes to minimize environmental impact and promote green technologies.
