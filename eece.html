Chemical and Petrochemical Engineering

It is concerned with processes in which matter and energy undergo change. The range of concerns is so broad that the chemical and Petrochemical engineering graduate is prepared for a variety of interesting and challenging employment opportunities.

CPE program in a E-JUST:

  • Introduction to Chemical Engineering
  • Thermodynamics
  • Heat Transfer
  • Chemical Reaction Engineering
  • Process Control and Instrumentation
  • Separation Processes
  • Transport Phenomena
  • Petrochemical Engineering
  • Petroleum Refining
  • Polymer Engineering
  • Process Safety and Hazard Analysis
  • Environmental Engineering in the Chemical Industry
  • Process Design and Optimization
  • Chemical Engineering Laboratory

Career prospects:

  • Chemical technician
  • Manufacturing production technician
  • Chemical plant operator
  • Pharmaceutical manufacturer
  • Petroleum engineer
  • Nuclear engineer

Environmental Engineering

Design systems for managing and cleaning municipal water supplies. Environmental engineers use engineering disciplines in developing solutions to problems of planetary health. Their work may involve concerns such as waste treatment, site remediation, and pollution control technology.

ENV programs in a E-JUST:

  • Fluid Mechanics
  • Water Resources Engineering
  • Air Pollution Control Engineering
  • Environmental Impact Assessment
  • Sustainable Design and Green Technologies
  • Geotechnical Engineering

Career prospects in a E-JUST:

Environmental engineers apply their broad understanding of engineering and the sciences, such as chemistry, biology, soil science and more, to improve human and ecological health through impactful engineering applications. They are involved in ensuring good air quality, controlling water pollution and remediation of wastes at contaminated sites. A degree in environmental engineering can lead to opportunities in industries, engineering consulting firms, government agencies and public interest groups. Environmental engineers can work in an office setting, laboratories or out in fieldwork.

Energy Resources Engineering

It is concerned with the production, transformation, and impacts of energy resources including renewables and fossil fuels. Oil and natural gas are important components of the current energy system due to their widespread use, economic importance, and contributions to climate change.

ERE programs in a E-JUST:

  • Renewable Energy Technologies
  • Fossil Fuel Energy Systems
  • Power Plant Engineering
  • Smart Grids and Energy Distribution
  • Energy Efficiency and Conservation
  • Environmental Impact of Energy Systems

Career prospects in a E-JUST:

Energy engineers will be highly sought after as countries work to meet rising energy demands while managing costs and pollution. There is greater public awareness regarding the need for clean, affordable, and sustainable energy sources, and employers are seeking skilled and knowledgeable engineers in these areas. Energy engineers, in most parts of the world, are highly valued and very well paid.
